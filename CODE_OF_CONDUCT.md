# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want Mere CLI to be a respectful, practical, and welcoming project for contributors and users.

Participants are expected to be considerate, direct, and collaborative. Disagreement is fine; harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language, sexualized attention, doxxing, or sustained disruption are not.

## Standards

Helpful participation includes:

- Being clear and kind when reporting bugs or asking questions.
- Respecting different experience levels and working styles.
- Keeping security reports private until maintainers have handled them.
- Avoiding logs, screenshots, or examples that expose tokens, customer data, workspace data, private URLs, or other sensitive information.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, threats, insults, or demeaning comments.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Publicly disclosing suspected vulnerabilities instead of following `SECURITY.md`.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions after maintainers ask for focus.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct. Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation for behavior that harms the project or community.

To report conduct concerns, contact the maintainers through a private Sawfwair channel. For suspected security vulnerabilities, follow `SECURITY.md`.
